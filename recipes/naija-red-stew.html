Nigerian Red Stew (with Rice)

Description

Nigerian Red Stew is a staple eaten all over West Africa with some ingredient variations based on culture and individual preferences. They are commonly made with blended tomatoes (sometimes with onions and peppers), cooked with some oil and some type of protein.

Depending on who is cooking it for you; the type of oil used, the other vegetables blended with the tomatoes, the consistency of the Red stew and the protein used to cook the stew varies.

Ingredients

  • Plum Tomatoes, chopped, divived
  • Tomato Paste
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Onions
  • Bell Peppers
  • Garlic cloves
  • Salt
  • Peppers
  • Various Spices (Cayenne suggested)
  • Vegetable Oil
  • White rice, cooked, for serving

Steps

  1. Cut/Chop/blend Plum Tomatoes, Onions, Bell Pappers, and Garlic.
  2. As you finish, start heating your vegatable oil in large pot over medium-high heat.
  3. Add Tomato/Veggie Blend, Paste, and Sauce to large pot.
  4. Let it cook for 20 minutes, until stew has thickened.
  5. While cooking, add spices and herbs.
  6. Remove stew from heat. Let it sit 10 minutes.
  7. Service with white rice & protein of your choice.
  8. Enjoy!
